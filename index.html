Structuring Your Equipment Finance

Selecting the correct financial structure is just as critical as choosing the lender. The two primary options for business equipment are chattel mortgages and finance leases. With a chattel mortgage, the business owns the asset immediately. This allows for the immediate claiming of GST on the purchase price and potential depreciation deductions over the life of the asset. Alternatively, a finance lease involves the lender retaining ownership while the business rents the asset. In this scenario, GST is paid on the monthly repayments rather than upfront.

The decision between these structures affects cash flow and tax outcomes. For example, a chattel mortgage might suit a business looking for long-term asset ownership and tax deductions, while a finance lease could appeal to those preferring lower monthly outlays and flexibility to upgrade equipment at the end of the term. Balloon payments are often available to reduce monthly commitments, though this results in a lump sum payment at the end. It is essential to consult with an accountant to determine which model aligns with your specific circumstances.

Eligible Assets and Equipment

The scope of assets eligible for finance is broad, covering almost any income-producing equipment. Commonly financed items include trucks, utes, and commercial fleets, which are vital for logistics and trade businesses. Manufacturing equipment, agricultural machinery such as tractors and harvesters, and construction excavators are also frequently funded through these channels. Medical equipment and technology upgrades for professional services firms represent another significant category, allowing businesses to upgrade capabilities without heavy upfront capital expenditure.

Both new and used assets can be financed, though lenders typically impose age limits to ensure security value. For instance, a used truck might need to be less than 15 years old at the end of the loan term. Minimum finance amounts generally start around $10,000, making this option accessible for smaller purchases as well as major capital investments. The asset itself usually serves as the primary security, simplifying the application process compared to unsecured loans. Whether purchasing from a dealer or via private sale, finance options are typically available to facilitate the transaction.

The Application Timeline

The timeline for securing finance is often faster than business owners expect. After an initial review of your trading history and income, a broker can seek pre-approval from a suitable lender, often within 24 hours. This conditional approval provides certainty when negotiating purchases. Following this, the submission of full documentation, such as bank statements and asset invoices, leads to formal approval.

From application to settlement, the process typically takes between 2 to 5 business days. This efficiency is driven by the broker's ability to present a clean, compliant application that matches the specific lending policy of the chosen institution. Common questions

Is using a finance broker free? For most business borrowers, yes. The lender pays the broker a commission on settlement, so the service is typically free to you. Any fee that does apply must be disclosed before you proceed.

What types of equipment can be financed? Almost any income-producing equipment can be financed, including trucks, machinery, construction equipment, medical devices, and technology. Both new and used assets are accepted, provided they meet the lender's age criteria.

How quickly can I get approval? Pre-approval is often available within 24 hours. Full approval and settlement usually take between 2 to 5 business days, depending on the complexity of the application and the speed of document provision.
